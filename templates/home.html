What is this?

A programming competition by and for RPI students. Coders of all levels and languages are welcome! Teams of up to four students work together for an entire semester to build an AI that will play our game.
Prize information coming soon!

To learn more about how to play and how to connect to the game, check out the full documentation and API.

This server, equipped with matchmaking capabilities, will be running 24/7 to allow teams to test their AIs against the rest of the competition. By winning games and improving your rating, you can fight your way to the top of our leaderboard!

Rules

The competition will be broken down into sections. Quals, Semifinals, Finals, and Boss mode. Code only has to be turned in if you are a finalist. Feel free to modify/improve/train your AI model in between the different competitions.
Quals

For Quals, you just have to beat our little dinky practice bot that just runs around doing random moves (except playing a bomb, so it won't kill itself!) Losing, or drawing it won't qualify you, you'll actually have to make it all the way over and kill it. Good luck!

Semifinals
Semifinals is a online competition that will will happen on November 30th. You will be given a time slot to compete as well as the link to connect to. This will be a round robin competition where the top 8 teams will move on to the final round.
Finals
Finals is bracketed single elimination where the winner will take all. We will be reserving a room, streaming the event, and providing refreshments. The Top three teams will each compete against each other for the cash prizes.

3rd place: $500
2nd place: $1,000
1st place: $1,500

Also, the winner of the Finals will be the only team allowed to move onto Boss Mode...
Boss Mode
Think you can write a better bot than UPE can? Then do it. The winner of the final round will be given the chance to face off against the bot developed by UPE. If your bot beats our bot (unlikely) then you will receive a bonus $1,000, for a total of $2,500

Timeline

Nov 2nd - Nov 20th: Quals
Nov 30th: Semifinals
Dec 5th: Finals + Boss Mode

How do I get involved?

  • If you're not familiar with Bomberman, rectify that immediately! It's a classic retro game that you may have played when you were a kid. The rules for this competition are somewhat different from traditional Bomberman, so go to Games > Training Mode to play around with it for yourself or read up in the Documentation!
  • Create an account from the Sign Up page, and get started making your AI! The details of testing your AI against the server are in the Documentation!
  • Have fun!

Learn

  • Read the Bomberman documentation
  • Play around with the game yourself

Develop

Code in your favorite language! The server and AIs communicate using HTTP requests (see the documentation for more details), and pretty much all languages can interface this way. Go nuts!

Test

Testing your AI against other AIs on the matchmaking server is super easy using our API! You can even test your AI against a sample practice bot that just moves randomly! Check out the Documentation for more details.

Compete

More on this coming later! There will be a seeding tournament and a main event that's open to entry to all interested RPI teams.
